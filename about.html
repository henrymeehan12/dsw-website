About DSW Cutting

Where We Came From

A Shop Reborn
in Steel City.

DSW's roots trace back to a knife shop that opened its doors in Birmingham in the 1960s — back when this city's steel mills ran around the clock and the air smelled like hot metal. The legacy of that era, of craftsmanship and heavy industry, is still very much alive here.

In 2004, Chris McIlvaine acquired the business and saw something others didn't: the potential to transform a legacy shop into one of the region's most capable precision metal fabrication operations. He invested in people, technology, and floor space — and kept investing.

Twenty years later, that bet has paid off. What began as a small cutting operation is now a 230,000 sq ft facility employing 80 people, running two shifts nearly 24/7, robotic bending cells, and tube laser systems capable of 40-foot cuts — all a few miles from the Sloss Furnace smokestacks that put Birmingham on the map.

How We Work

We're a Product Partner.
Not Just a Vendor.

Since 2004, DSW has been supporting manufacturers by producing the parts they need — accurately, affordably, and on time. That's the baseline. What separates us is everything that happens around the part.

Our sales team has real manufacturing experience. When you call DSW, you're talking to someone who understands your drawing, your material, your tolerances — and can tell you if there's a better way to make it before the quote goes out. That's DFM guidance most shops don't offer.

Our quality program includes first-piece sampling verification on every new part — so you know what you ordered is exactly what you're getting before we run the full production quantity. No surprises at receiving.

01
Manufacturing-Experienced Sales
Our sales engineers understand fabrication. They review drawings, suggest process improvements, and flag DFM issues at quoting — not after the order is placed.

First-Piece Sampling Program
Every new part goes through first-piece verification before full production. Your parts are to spec before we run quantity. No exceptions.

Two-Shift, Near 24/7 Production
Two shifts keep DSW running nearly 24/7. Automated material towers and robotic bending cells maximize throughput across both shifts — competitive lead times, even on high volume.

Laser, tube laser, bending, welding, and finishing under one roof — managed by a single point of contact. Fewer vendors. Fewer handoffs. Better parts.
